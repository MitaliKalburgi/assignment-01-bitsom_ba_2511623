## Vector DB Use Case

A traditional keyword-based database search would not be sufficient for searching large legal documents such as 500-page contracts. Keyword-based systems rely on exact word matching, which means they may fail to retrieve relevant information if the wording in the document differs from the user’s query. For example, a lawyer searching for “termination clauses” might miss sections that use alternative phrases such as “contract termination conditions” or “agreement ending terms.” This limitation reduces the effectiveness of keyword-based search in complex legal contexts.

In contrast, a vector database enables semantic search by converting both the query and the document content into numerical embeddings. These embeddings capture the meaning of the text rather than just the exact words used. As a result, even if the wording is different, the system can still identify relevant sections based on similarity in meaning.

For a law firm, this approach significantly improves search accuracy and efficiency. Lawyers can ask questions in natural language and quickly retrieve the most relevant parts of a document without manually scanning hundreds of pages. The vector database plays a crucial role by storing embeddings and enabling fast similarity comparisons, making the search process more intelligent and context-aware.

Therefore, a vector database is far more suitable than a traditional keyword-based system for this use case.
